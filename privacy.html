Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 2026

Introduction

Welcome to Spin A Wheel ("we", "our", "us"). We run an interactive random wheel spinner tool accessible entirely online via your browser. Your privacy matters to us, which is why our application is engineered to safeguard your personal space. This Privacy Policy details the types of non-personally identifiable metrics tracked and how the site handles your configuration states.

Information We Do Not Collect

Spin A Wheel is 100% free and does not require you to construct an account, login, or input personal identifiers like your name, email address, or billing context to use our random wheels. Any list values, student arrays, customized names, or color settings you input into our interactive input field parameters are processed immediately on your device in local temporary variables and are never transferred to our global web servers.

Analytical & Infrastructure Tracking

To audit infrastructure balance and observe user-base retention behaviors, we drop client-side scripts to document technical session activity. This encompasses:

  • Cookies & Identifiers: We utilize third-party packages, specifically Google Analytics (gtag.js), to process generalized geographic regions, active device models, browser classifications, and core click paths.
  • Log Files: Standard web analytics track server requests, timing vectors, referring domains, and generalized connection telemetry. This metadata is strictly evaluated as aggregated statistics rather than tied to identifiable people.

Third-Party Services

Our website utilizes industry-standard metrics platforms to understand engagement profiles:

  • Google Tag Manager / Google Analytics: These networks apply behavioral analytical identifiers to verify unique traffic ratios. You can easily switch off cookie preservation mechanisms directly via individual web browser control consoles.

Data Retention Lifecycle

All temporary configuration properties (such as user-built item labels or spin historical lists) are tracked inside standard frontend window variables or isolated runtime arrays. Refreshing your active window instantly clears this operational memory pool.

Contact Architecture

If you have clarification questions, architecture recommendations, or regulatory policy inquires regarding the operations of our system, you are encouraged to connect directly through our public code repository channels or open-source community portals.
